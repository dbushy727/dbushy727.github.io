Biography

Roots
I first started learning code when I joined the hacker bootcamp, General Assembly. I spent anywhere between 10-14 hours a day melting my brain with Ruby, SQL, Javascript, etc., all the while downing massive quantities of coffee and cereal bars.

Then, I developed the thirst (not coffee). The thirst for more. (definitely not more coffee) Since then, I have been non-stop coding. Refactoring old techniques to follow best practices and conventions as well as mastering new abilities.

A passion had been born.
Music
Music has always played a big role in my life. It started when I was fourteen and got my hands on a music program called Fruity Loops. Since then, I have been learning different musical instruments like the Guitar and Bass Guitar, writing music, fine-tuning recordings and picking up different skills along the way.

It was only fitting that after I learned to code, I landed a Full Stack Applications Developer role at the nations leader in Mechanical Licensing and Rights Management, The Harry Fox Agency (HFA).
Experience
Working at HFA, I have been fortunate enough to be at a company that is going through a technology overhaul. Due to the natural state of an evolving company, I get to design, build and implement tons of applications.

I primarily work in a LAMP Stack (Linux-Apache-MySQL-PHP) environment with Code Igniter on Amazon Web Services (AWS) running Ubuntu/CentOS. We deal with a lot of data processing, and I also get to explore different Javascript libraries such as D3.js for various needs.
